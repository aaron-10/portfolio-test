Model roller coaster

2021 - Present

Overview

I've been wanting to build a model roller coaster for a long time. There is a lot possible with a rather cheap 3D printed (Ender 3) with some small nozzles. At the moment I'm experimenting with different track, bogie and cart designs, inspired by amazing projects such as the model coasters by Dan Fritsche. One day I want to build an entire model coaster, but I am trying to get step by step, by first getting all the seperate parts working. At the moment I can already create 3D printed track, but I'm working on automating track generation to make it more feasible to model larger amounts of track.

The wheel bogies have two main wheels, two side wheels and one upstop wheel, all containing a tiny 3mm OD bearing. It took quite some iterations to properly finetune the designs of all the tiny parts that need to fit together perfectly and still move freely.

The wheels can be 3D printed slowly using a 0.2mm nozzle. The outer surface is nowhere near smooth enough, so by connecting the wheels to the axis of a Dremel, the surface is smoothed using very fine grit sandpaper.


The remaining parts are also printed with 0.2mm and 0.3mm nozzles, and are design to be press fitted together, only needing a tiny drop of superglue to stay together. The axles are simply made from 1mm steel wire and they are also superglued in place.

The bogies connect to the carts via a 3mm bolt though a 5mm OD bearing, such that they can yaw freely. The plan is to connect the carts into a train using universal couplings.

So far I have only actually built the front cart. The front and back of the cart can rotate with respect to eachother thanks to a small bearing, which allows the required degree of freedom for the carts to follow the track. It runs really smoothly on the track, even sideways, upside down and on a banked track section. The next steps are to realize the rest of the train, and creating more track sections for testing.
